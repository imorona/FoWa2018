The Problem



India is home to the largest undernourished population in the world, and Isabella Morona and Allison Zhu were exposed to the prominence of this issue when they visited undeveloped areas.

While malnutrition is a leading issue in India, the United States is facing the increasing issue of obesity and food waste. In the United States, about 40 percent of the total food supply is wasted, with Phillips Andover Academy contributing about 390lbs each day. The conveyor belt system we have in Paresky Commons leaves a false impression that food waste disappears, and the buffet setup leaves another impression that we can waste food because the school is paying for it.

Our Solution


The Food Waste Challenge is a Phillips Academy student-led event that encourages the community to reduce food waste on campus while assisting malnourished communities.

On April 20th, student volunteers will be counting the total number of clean plates that pass through the four food disposal stations in the dining hall from 5-7pm. The goal of this challenge is to reduce the amount of food waste in Paresky Commons within one mealtime and to set an example for future meals.

A fundraising event is paired with this challenge, and volunteers will be collecting donations in the lobby of Paresky starting Friday, April 13th. We encourage members of the Andover community to donate flat amounts and/or pledge money per clean plate counted during the event. Donations will be collected after the number of clean plates has been tallied and released, and all the proceeds will go towards Bread and Roses in Lawrence.
